Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We’ve got answers.

If you don’t see your question here, please reach out — we’re happy to help.

How often are the treatments? How many per season?

Our treatments are scheduled every 3–4 weeks during peak mosquito activity. A typical season includes about six visits, from spring through fall, to keep mosquito and tick populations consistently suppressed.

Do you service my area?

We currently serve much of northern Delaware and nearby communities, including:

  • New Castle County, Delaware
  • Kent County, Maryland
  • Cecil County, Maryland
  • Chester County, Pennsylvania

Not sure if you’re in our service area? Send us your address and we’ll confirm availability.

What do I need to do after the treatment?

You can improve results by removing standing water around your yard — look for clogged gutters, birdbaths, flowerpot saucers, toys, and other water-holding containers. Keeping grass and vegetation trimmed also reduces mosquito hiding spots and helps treatments work more effectively.

After we treat, we recommend allowing at least one hour for the product to dry before people and pets re-enter treated areas (longer if conditions are very humid or shady).

Are these products safe?

We use EPA-registered products that are labeled for residential mosquito and tick control. When applied correctly by trained technicians, they are considered safe for people, pets, and the environment.

As a precaution, we ask that people and pets stay indoors until the application is completely dry — typically about an hour.

Is this service safe for native insects and pollinators?

Our treatments are designed to target the areas where mosquitoes and ticks rest — such as turf edges, dense shrubs, and ornamental beds. We avoid spraying blooms or flowering plants where bees and other pollinators typically forage.

While any insecticide can be harmful if a pollinator is directly contacted, we follow best practices to minimize that risk. As beekeepers and pollinator advocates ourselves, we take that responsibility seriously.

Do you treat commercial properties and events?

Yes. In addition to residential properties, we provide services for commercial sites and one-time events such as weddings, outdoor parties, and corporate gatherings. Contact us with the event date and location so we can hold a spot on the schedule.

What other services do you offer?

Through Mosquito Solutions and our partnered brands, we can connect you with additional lawn and outdoor services. If you have specific needs beyond mosquito and tick control, let us know and we’ll point you in the right direction.

How long does a typical visit take?

Most standard residential applications take 20–30 minutes, depending on the size and complexity of the property. Larger or heavily wooded lots may take a bit longer.

Do I need to be home for each treatment?

You do not need to be home as long as we have access to the areas we’re treating. Many of our customers are at work during service. We’ll leave a door hanger or send a notification so you know we were there.

What happens if it rains?

Light rain after an application usually does not affect results once the product has dried. If there is heavy rain immediately after a treatment and you feel performance has been reduced, please contact us — we’ll review the situation and make it right.

How much does the service cost?

Pricing depends on factors such as property size, landscaping, and whether you’re signing up for a full season or a single event. The best way to get accurate pricing is to request a free quote.

You can fill out our quote form or call us at (302) 832-8040 and we’ll provide clear, upfront pricing.

Can I pause or cancel service?

Yes. We don’t require long-term contracts. If you need to pause service temporarily or cancel for any reason, just let us know before the next scheduled visit and we’ll update your account.
